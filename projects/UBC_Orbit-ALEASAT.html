Project Background

UBC Orbit is a satellite design team comprising over 100 undergraduate and graduate students from a variety of disciplines. ALEASAT is a 1U (10cm x 10cm x 10cm) LEO (low Earth orbit) observation satellite that can be used by radio amateurs in disaster monitor and relief opeartions through image transmission directly from the satellite to the ground station. ALEASAT is currently being developed by student members of UBC Orbit and SFU Satellite Design Team, and the project is supported by UBC, the Radio Amateurs of Canada, and the European Space Agency's Fly Your Satellite! Programme (ESA). The project is still in the process of building and testing with an aim to launch in a few years.

  4. performing system integration between the Structure and the Payload Subsystem (specifically designing the on-boarding camera mounting system).
