Project Background

The Piano-Playing Robot is a two-year Capstone project self-sponsered by a team of four along with the ENPH Project Lab., currently working-in-progress.

The goal of the project is to provide accessile, live piano music at a professional-level caliber without the need for a real-life musician. The project aims to design a robotic hand mechanism capable of replicating human pianists' ability to play different dynamics, articulations, and pitches on a standard upright piano.

In the first year of the project timeline, we have developed the project proposal, and aim to develop one robotic finger capable of playing dynamics from pp (very soft) to ff (very strong) and different note lengths. Finger-to-hand extension, lateral motion (pitches), and audio calibration will be the focus of next year.

Actuator Design

Once we acquire the necessary position/velocity data to generate a motion profile, we need an actuator to excecute these instructions, and the actuator we have selected is the voice coil.

A rotary voice coil is a type of motor containg a coil and permanent magnets. Its basic principle follows the Lorentz Force Law, where the magnetic flux generated from two permanent magnets interacts with the current-carrying coil, producing a magnetic force. The magnitude of the force can be calculated by: $$F = nBIℓ$$ where

  • n = number of loops in the coil
  • B = magnetic field strength
  • I = current
  • ℓ = length of one side of the coil

Figure 2: Rotary voice coil setup with magnetic force generated running in the -x direction.

As shown in the diagram above, we see that when magnetic flux is running from the north pole to the south pole, and two sides of the coil have current running in the opposite directions. By Right-hand rule, we can tell that the magnetic force generated from both magnets is towards the -x direction. As soon as the coil with out-of-page current reaches the red magnet, the magnetic force will switch direction, allowing the coil to move toward the +x direction.

Figure 3: A voice coil schematic diagram showing current running in CCW direction and magnetic field in reversed directions, generating a magentic force in the downward direction by the Right-hand Rule.

Figure 3 shows the schematic diagram of another paper utilizing a voice coil to achieve downward forces to press a pinao key. With this setup in mind, we have developed our own version of rotary voice coil to achieve the pressing and releasing of the piano key.

Figure 4: CAD model of the rotary voice coil design.

The figure above is the CAD model of our actuator design. The finger and coil is one solid body connected to the base using two spring steel flexure bearings, and two permanent magnets are mounted at the back of the base. When there is current running through the coil, the finger will be moved to the desired position by the magnetic force; when current is turned off, the spring force in the flexure bearings will restore the finger back to it's original position, achieving the release action.
